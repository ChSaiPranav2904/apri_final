About Liver Health

Understanding the liver’s role in health and disease

The liver is one of the most vital organs in the human body. It regulates metabolism, detoxifies harmful substances, stores nutrients, and aids digestion. Maintaining liver health is essential to overall well-being, and early screening for liver damage is critical in preventing long-term complications such as fibrosis or cirrhosis.

Range of APRI Values for various liver conditions

The range of APRI scores can correlate with the severity of liver damage or fibrosis in various conditions.

1.Good Liver (No Significant Fibrosis)/n APRI < 0.5/n Interpretation: Normal liver function or minimal fibrosis (F0–F1)/n 2.Fibrosis (Significant Liver Scarring)/n APRI 0.5 – 1.5/n Interpretation: Moderate to significant fibrosis (F2–F3)/n 3.Cirrhosis (Severe Liver Damage)/n APRI > 1.5/n Interpretation: High likelihood of cirrhosis (F4)/n

About This Project

A collaborative tool built with clinical and academic synergy

This APRI Calculator is the outcome of a joint initiative between students of BITS Pilani, Hyderabad Campus, and clinicians from Yashoda Hospitals. It provides a quick, accessible way to assess liver fibrosis risk using AST levels and platelet counts — no expensive imaging or invasive biopsy required.
